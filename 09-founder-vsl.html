Dear fellow pool guy,

My dad ran 54 pools out of a Chevy in Round Rock from 1989 until the day his back went out. He kept everything on a yellow legal pad and a map he drew in pencil. When he handed me the route, he handed me the pad. True story.

It took me nine months to lose four customers to chemistry I didn’t see coming. I was 31, working sixteen-hour days, and the software I bought cost me $218 a month and crashed every time I drove past a live oak. I called it “modern,” but my dad’s pad was better at its job than the app was at its job.

this part still bothers me: The only reason I built PoolOps was because nobody else would. Every pool software meeting I sat in ended with a guy in a blazer saying “we’re horizontal — pools are a use case.” Pools aren’t a use case. Pools are the whole thing.

  1. PoolOps will always work offline. A pool cage is not a network problem, it’s a pool problem.
  2. PoolOps will always charge one flat monthly rate. No per-pool, no per-tech, no surprises on a Tuesday.
  3. PoolOps will always answer the phone. When I say founder access, I mean my cell.
  4. PoolOps will always be built by a team that has stood in a backyard at 6 a.m. looking at algae.
