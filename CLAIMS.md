# PoolOps · Claims Registry

**Purpose.** A single source of truth for every number, percentage, testimonial, comparison, and guarantee that appears in PoolOps marketing — ads, VSLs, websites, email, sales calls, decks, social.

**Rule.** Nothing goes into a piece of marketing that is not in this file. New claim? Add it here *first*, with a source. If you can't source it, it ships in Tier C or it doesn't ship.

**Maintained by.** Brandon Calloway (founder) — one editor keeps it consistent. Changes reviewed monthly against actual product data.

---

## ⚠️ Current product stage · pre-launch

**As of 2026-04-19, PoolOps has zero external paying users.** The founder — Brandon Calloway — owns and operates two pool service companies (Pool Cleaning Dude, Tri-State Aquatic Solutions) covering roughly 50 customer routes between them. PoolOps is being built and used internally against those 50 routes. That is the entire current dataset.

**What this means for marketing:**

- Every "N operators" / "N customers" / "trusted by X teams" claim is **zero**. Do not inflate.
- Every "saves X%", "cuts X by Y", "customers report Z" claim is **aspiration, not evidence**. Word it as intent ("built to cut drive time") or target ("goal: 30%+ on our routes by Q3"), never as a customer outcome.
- Testimonials do not exist. Labeled "illustrative" at best, omitted at worst.
- Partner/enterprise logos do not exist. Omit the strip.
- The story we *can* tell honestly is powerful: **pool service owner, 50 routes, building the tool he wished existed, early-access slots open, founder pricing, direct access to Brandon**. Lead with that.

**What we can say today:**

1. Founder is a real pool service operator running 50 routes.
2. The product is being dog-fooded against those routes daily.
3. Early-access / founding-operator slots are open with founder pricing.
4. The problem we're solving (routes + chemistry + callbacks) is real — cited with industry-typical ranges, not PoolOps data.

## The three tiers

| Tier | What it is | Where it can appear | How it's written |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A · Facts** | Pricing, packaging, feature existence, team size. Under direct control. | Anywhere, freely | Plain. "$79/mo flat." |
| **B · Measured** | Performance claim backed by real data — PoolOps data once we have it, or a cited external study. | Anywhere — must carry qualifier | With scope. "Avg. 38% less drive time across our first N operators, Q3 2026." |
| **C · Illustrative / aspirational** | Goals, narrative scenes, example figures, composite stories, product intent. | Long-form only (VSLs, landing pages). Never as a headline stat on an ad. | Labeled. "Our target · not a customer figure." |

**While pre-launch, Tier B is effectively empty.** Every quantitative performance claim lives in Tier C until we have data from real external users. Move claims up to Tier B one at a time, with the data, as they graduate.

## Approved copy snippets (copy-paste library)

### Founder / pre-launch lead
> Hi, I'm Brandon Calloway. I run two pool service companies with about 50 customer routes between them. I'm building PoolOps because nobody else was building the tool I needed. Early-access slots are open — you get founder pricing, my direct number, and a voice in what ships next.

### Pricing line — short
> $79/month flat. Up to 3 techs, 200 pools. No per-pool fees. Founder pricing locked for early-access operators.

### Pricing line — long
> PoolOps is $79 per month, flat — up to 3 technicians and 200 pools. No per-pool fees. No per-tech fees. 14-day free trial; no card required. Early-access operators keep $79 permanently.

### Positioning — one sentence
> Pool service management built for 2–15 technician operators — route optimization, chemistry tracking, and invoicing in one place. Built by a pool service owner for pool service owners.

### Chemistry angle — honest pre-launch
> PoolOps is the only pool-service software designed around chemistry tracking — built to catch the pH drift and chlorine slide that become the 7am green-pool call. Industry-typical callback costs run $150–$500 each; the product goal is to prevent most of them. Real-world callback reduction data will follow our first cohort of external pilot operators.

### Route angle — honest pre-launch
> PoolOps is being built to reorder your day so driving time stops eating your profit. Target: a meaningful reduction in daily drive hours by the first week. Early-access operators set the benchmark — your before/after becomes the proof.

### Guarantee — offer line
> Try PoolOps free for 14 days. If it doesn't save you time, email me — I'll refund the first month, and I want to hear why.

### Early-access invitation
> Early access is first-come. I'm taking 20 founding operators at the current $79/mo rate, which stays with you as long as you run PoolOps. In exchange, I ask for five minutes of feedback whenever I ship a change.

## Standard disclosure footer (long-form)

Every VSL and landing page carries this near the footer:

> *PoolOps is pre-launch. Performance figures on this page describe product goals and illustrative examples, not customer outcomes. The founder operates two pool service companies and is building PoolOps against his own 50 routes. Real customer data will replace these figures as the first external pilot operators go live.*

Short-form (Facebook/Instagram static ads) may omit the disclosure but cannot state quantitative performance claims without a "goal" / "target" / "illustrative" qualifier on the creative itself.
